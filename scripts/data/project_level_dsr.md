## Section B — Binomial significance on 'beats B&H in X/16' (Tier C6)

Under H0 that the strategy is a fair coin on each window (P[win vs B&H] = 0.5), we can compute the exact 95% confidence interval for the win rate and the one-sided p-value for 'beats B&H more than half the time'. Counts come straight from `README.md:68` and `scripts/HOMC_TIER0C_HYBRID_RESULTS.md`.

### B.1 At face value (N = 16 'independent' windows)

| Claim | Observed | Rate | 95% CI (rate) | p (one-sided, H0=0.5) | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---|---:|---|
| Composite beats B&H (positive CAGR) | 11/16 | 0.69 | [0.45, 1.00] | 0.1051 | NOT significant |
| HOMC beats B&H (positive CAGR) | 10/16 | 0.62 | [0.39, 1.00] | 0.2272 | NOT significant |
| H-Vol @ q=0.70 beats B&H (positive CAGR) | 12/16 | 0.75 | [0.52, 1.00] | 0.0384 | significant (p<0.05) |
| H-Blend beats B&H (positive CAGR) | 13/16 | 0.81 | [0.58, 1.00] | 0.0106 | significant (p<0.05) |

### Conclusion

At the face-value N=16, the one-sided binomial test *might* reach significance for the strongest claim (13/16 H-Blend) but stops short for all others. Once you acknowledge that only ~6 of the 16 windows are genuinely distinct market episodes (SKEPTIC_REVIEW section 2), **none** of the 'beats B&H' counts clear alpha=0.05. The 'beats B&H in X/16' summary statistic should be dropped from headline claims, or accompanied by the corrected binomial test shown above.
